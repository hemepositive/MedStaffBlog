A refresher on CMS Conditions of Participation requirements (42 CFR § 482.13(e)) for physicians and APPs ordering restraint or seclusion for violent or self-destructive behavior. These requirements apply to all Medicare/Medicaid-participating hospitals.

<div class="key-message">

Seclusion may only be used for violent or self-destructive behavior — non-violent restraints follow a separate, less stringent pathway. The requirements below apply specifically to violent/self-destructive situations.

</div>

## Critical timeline after restraints are applied

| Step | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| **Order required — immediate** | Physician must place a specific, time-limited order. An RN may enter a verbal order. No standing or PRN orders permitted. |
| **Face-to-face evaluation — within 1 hour** | Physician or trained RN must see the patient in person. Assess situation, reaction, medical/behavioral condition, and continued need. |
| **Consult MD — as soon as possible** | If an RN completed the 1-hour evaluation, the attending physician must be consulted immediately after. |
| **Adult order limit — 4 hours** | Each adult order expires after 4 hours. Orders may be renewed up to a 24-hour total before reassessment is required. |
| **Physician reassessment — required at 24 hours** | Physician must personally see and assess the patient before any new order can be written. |

## Time-limited order maximums

| Patient population | Age range | Max order duration | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Adults | 18 years and older | **4 hours** | Up to 6 renewal orders before 24-hour physician reassessment is required |
| Children / adolescents | 9 to 17 years | **2 hours** | Up to 12 renewal orders before 24-hour physician reassessment is required |
| Young children | Under 9 years | **1 hour** | Up to 24 renewal orders before 24-hour physician reassessment is required |

## The 1-hour face-to-face: what must be assessed

- **Immediate situation:** what triggered the restraint and the patient's current environment
- **Reaction to intervention:** how the patient is responding physically and behaviorally
- **Medical & behavioral condition:** vital signs, mental status, relevant comorbidities
- **Continued necessity:** explicit clinical decision to continue or terminate restraint/seclusion

## Who can order and evaluate

- **Order restraint:** physician authorized by hospital policy and state law. An RN may place a verbal order.
- **1-hour evaluation:** physician, or trained RN (per §482.13(f))
- **If RN evaluates:** attending MD must be consulted as soon as possible
- **If a non-attending physician ordered:** attending physician must be notified ASAP
- **24-hour reassessment:** physician only — cannot be delegated to a nurse

## Monitoring requirements

- **Continuous monitoring:** by physician, or trained staff at intervals per hospital policy
- **Simultaneous restraint + seclusion:** requires continuous face-to-face OR audio+video monitoring in close proximity
- **Earliest discontinuation:** restraint must be removed at the earliest possible time, regardless of order duration
- **Trained staff must:** recognize respiratory/circulatory distress and positional asphyxia risk

## Absolute prohibitions

- **No standing orders:** every restraint episode requires a new, specific physician order
- **No PRN orders:** as-needed orders are explicitly prohibited under §482.13(e)(6)
- **No coercion/discipline:** restraints may not be used as punishment, convenience, or retaliation
- **Least restrictive required:** must use the least restrictive intervention that is effective

## Required medical record documentation (§482.13(e)(16))

- **1-hour evaluation findings** — full findings of the face-to-face medical and behavioral evaluation, including clinical assessments and decision rationale
- **Behavior & intervention** — description of patient behavior and the specific type of restraint or seclusion intervention applied
- **Alternatives attempted** — less restrictive interventions tried before restraint was applied, and why they were insufficient
- **Clinical justification** — patient's condition or symptom(s) that warranted the use of restraint or seclusion
- **Patient response** — how the patient responded to the intervention and rationale for continued use if still applied
- **Care plan modification** — written modification to the patient's plan of care reflecting the restraint or seclusion decision

<div class="takeaway">

Every violent/self-destructive restraint episode needs a new, specific order (never PRN or standing), a 1-hour face-to-face evaluation, and complete documentation covering findings, behavior, alternatives tried, justification, response, and care plan changes. Physician reassessment is required — and cannot be delegated — at 24 hours.

</div>
