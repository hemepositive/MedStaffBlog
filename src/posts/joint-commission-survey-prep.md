Our Joint Commission survey is approaching in March, and preparation is essential. Surveyors will be on-site observing processes, reviewing documentation, and speaking with staff and physicians. **Every physician and APP should be familiar with the topics below**, which represent the areas most likely to come up during your interactions with surveyors.

We have prepared a quick reference guide that covers these topics and much more. **Please download and review it before the survey.**

## Top 5 Physician Topics

### 1. History and Physicals

- H&Ps must be completed **within 24 hours of admission** and **prior to any invasive procedure**, as outlined by the Medical Record Policy.
- If an H&P was completed prior to admission, it must be **no more than 30 days old** and must be updated.
- An H&P that is **31 days old or greater is not accepted**. Day 1 is the day it was written.
- When updating an H&P, the following must be documented: that the H&P was reviewed, another examination was completed, and whether there were no changes or what changes were noted.

### 2. Informed Consent

- Informed consent must include **risks, benefits, and alternatives** for blood administration, anesthesia/sedation, and procedures/surgery.
- Informed consent can **only be obtained by a physician**.
- It must be **documented in the medical record prior to patient procedures** via office notes, pre-procedural informed consent, History and Physical, Progress Notes, "Yellow Sheet", etc.

### 3. Site Marking

- For cases involving **laterality or levels**, the physician performing the procedure must verify the surgical site with the patient and mark the site with the word **"YES"**.
- Use an **indelible, hypoallergenic, latex-free skin marker**, placed as close as possible to the incision site and visible after draping.
- Site marking should take place **with the patient involved, awake, and aware** whenever possible.

### 4. Time Out

Surveyors will be observing the time out process during procedures. Make sure the following elements are in place:

- A time out is completed **immediately prior to any invasive procedure** requiring patient authorization (consent) and must involve **all relevant staff in the room** to be in agreement.
- Required in **all patient care areas**, including the Operating Room and procedural areas.
- Must verify: **Correct Patient, Correct Procedure, Correct Place** on the patient's body.
- A standardized checklist of required items must be available, including relevant documentation (H&P, correct consent forms), informed consent, and pre-anesthesia assessment.
- Correct images and results must be properly labeled and displayed.
- Any needed blood products, supplies, devices, implants, or special equipment must be confirmed.

### 5. Verbal Orders (Face to Face)

- Verbal orders are **not allowed** unless the physician is physically unable to input the order.
- When a verbal or telephone order is necessary, it must be **dated and timed** when written, along with the signature of who wrote the order.
- The physician must **sign, date, and time** the order within **48 hours**. The physician's signature must also be dated and timed.
- **All entries** in the medical record must be timed, dated, signed, and have the author identified.

## Be Prepared

If a surveyor approaches you, remember to be open, honest, and professional. Know your department's policies and be ready to explain the processes you follow daily. If you are unsure about something, it is okay to say so and direct them to your department leader.
