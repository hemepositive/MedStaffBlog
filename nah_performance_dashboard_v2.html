Shift-Normalized & Role-Normalized Z-Score

We calculate Z-scores by looking at the provider's rate per shift ($Orders/Shift$) relative only to their direct role cohort. Outliers are defined as having an absolute Z-score of $\ge 1.5$ standard deviations from their role mean ($|Z| \ge 1.5$).

High Volume Outliers (Z ≥ +1.5)

Providers placing consults at an unusually high rate compared to peers in the same role.

Low Volume Outliers (Z ≤ -1.5)

Providers placing consults at an unusually low rate compared to peers in the same role.

Executive Summary

Q1 2026 Provider Consult Ordering Intensity & Practice Variation Analysis

To: Hospital Leadership  |  From: Clinical Performance & Analytics  |  Date: June 9, 2026

1. Executive Summary & Core Conclusion

This analysis evaluates consult ordering patterns across clinical roles in Quarter 1, 2026. The dataset encompasses 5,698 consult orders placed by 62 active providers across 3,068.7 total shifts.

  • Primary Finding: Job role is the single largest driver of consult ordering frequency. Admitters place consults at a rate of 2.79 orders per shift, representing a 61.3% higher intensity than Standard providers (1.73 orders per shift).
  • Practice Variation: Significant variation exists within cohorts. Several providers exhibit ordering rates statistically higher or lower than their direct peers. These outliers have been successfully isolated using role-normalized Z-scores to guide targeted clinical audits.

Conclusions: Admitters and Nocturnists represent high-throughput admission and triage workflows with significantly higher consult ordering rates per shift. Medical Directors exhibit the lowest ordering rates, reflecting their focus on administrative duties.

3. Methodology & Statistical Rigor

To ensure clinical and statistical validity, the following methodologies were applied:

Data Cleansing & Deduplication

Raw casing mismatches were resolved in the source workbook: The provider entry BROWN (Allison Brown, CRNP) was standardized to Brown to align with the rest of the dataset. This successfully merged their records, reducing the unique name count from 63 to 62.

Shift-Standardization (Rate per Shift)

Comparing raw consult counts is misleading because schedules vary. We calculated ordering intensity as:
Ordering Rate = Total Consult Orders / Total Shifts

Role-Normalization (Z-Score Analysis)

Evaluating all providers on a single global average is statistically invalid because an Admitter is expected to order more than a Standard provider. Instead, we calculated Z-scores within each specific role cohort:
Z = (Provider's Rate - Role Mean Rate) / Role Standard Deviation

  • Z ≥ +1.5 (High Outlier): Ordering at a rate at least 1.5 standard deviations above their direct peers (potential over-utilization).
  • Z ≤ -1.5 (Low Outlier): Ordering at a rate at least 1.5 standard deviations below their direct peers (potential under-utilization).

4. Cohort Limits & Qualitative Evaluation

Statistical Notice: Some clinical roles have sample sizes too small for Z-score analysis: Medical Director (N=2), Med Dir Madison (N=1), and Madison Only (N=2) cohorts do not have enough providers to calculate a stable standard deviation. Z-scores for these roles are marked as N/A on the dashboard. They should be monitored qualitatively against historical baselines rather than via statistical models, as any calculated Z-score in a cohort of 1 or 2 is a mathematical artifact rather than a true measure of practice variation.
